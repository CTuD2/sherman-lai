Research & Practice

An integrative approach to Chinese medicine

Clinical background, professional presentations, and the philosophy behind Lai's practice.

Clinical background

Lai has practised acupuncture and Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) in Guelph since 1986, and has written and presented on TCM theory and clinical observation at Canadian and international conferences, including meetings co-sponsored by the World Health Organization. His practice centres on Traditional Chinese Medical theory: the idea that illness reflects an imbalance in the body's energetic and physiological systems, and that treatment should address the underlying pattern of imbalance rather than a symptom in isolation.

He has cited peer-reviewed research, including University of Toronto studies on acupuncture's physiological effects, in explaining how acupuncture is understood to stimulate the body's own pain-modulating and anti-inflammatory responses. He has also been a long-standing advocate for professional standards in acupuncture and TCM, contributing his clinical experience to Ontario's regulatory process for the professions in 2003–2004.

Conference presentations

Over the course of his career, Lai has presented clinical observation reports and literature reviews at TCM and acupuncture conferences in Canada and the United States, including:

  • 1995Presented clinical observation data at the World Association of Chinese Medicine conference, Toronto
  • 1996Presented a comparative study of acupuncture and acupuncture-with-cupping for musculoskeletal pain at the Fourth World Conference on Acupuncture, New York (co-sponsored by the World Health Organization)
  • 1996Presented a second clinical observation report at the same WHO-co-sponsored conference in New York
  • 1997Published a clinical observation on an herbal approach to osteoarthritis, later used as a practitioner reference
  • 2000Presented at the Toronto International Traditional Chinese Medicine Conference
  • 2003–2006Authored a series of clinical observation reports and literature reviews on TCM approaches to immune support, stress-related conditions, and menopausal symptoms
These are clinical observation reports and conference presentations reflecting Lai's own case series and clinical practice, not independent, peer-reviewed, placebo-controlled trials. They are shared here as a record of his professional and academic activity, not as claims that any product or protocol treats or cures a specific disease. Anyone considering a health decision should consult a qualified, licensed practitioner.

Practice philosophy

Lai has often described his approach as treating "the root, not just the branch" — using TCM diagnostic frameworks such as Qi, blood, and organ-system balance alongside dietary and lifestyle guidance, in coordination with a patient's other medical care rather than as a replacement for it. He is a frequent point of reference for Western practitioners seeking to understand TCM's diagnostic logic, and has spoken at medical institutes on the relationship between TCM theory and Western clinical science.
